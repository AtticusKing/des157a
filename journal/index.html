Form Design Research

As a Graphic Designer, this article on form design resonates with me in terms of the balance between aesthetics and functionality, as it can be a tough mental battle between artistic creativity and a good user experience (UX). For example, the emphasis on minimizing input fields and following the "Less is More" theory aligns with the design principle of simplicity, ensuring a visually clean and efficient form. The article's author, Salim Ansari, cautions against assumptions and highlights the need for thorough user research, a principle applicable to graphic design as well, where understanding the target audience is absolutely crucial. Additionally, I feel that the mention of accessibility considerations serves as a valuable reminder to designers to create designs that cater to users of all abilities.

Article

The UC Davis library website exemplifies best practices of form creation by understanding the needs of the user and categorizing the website first into a number of spaces, using icons as cleanly styled buttons. Within these, there is a general search bar with an “advanced search” option and a simple checkbox for searching in UC Davis libraries only. Ultimately it exemplifies best practices because it is so easily navigated.
