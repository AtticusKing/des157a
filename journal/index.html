Game Design Research

One key takeaway for me was Dori's discussion on UI positions, particularly in mobile games. His point about considering the comfort-to-reach area for interactive elements like store buttons and ads makes perfect sense. It's crucial to strategically place these elements where users are most likely to interact with them, maximizing monetization without compromising user experience. The section on sliders also caught my attention, as it addresses a common challenge in game design: presenting a lot of content within limited screen real estate. Dori's tips on making partly visible items within sliders and animating their launch to guide users are practical and insightful strategies that I'll definitely keep in mind for future projects.

Visual Thinking Strategies Research

I think the concept of slowing down and closely reading images and text is great in this world of increasing speeds. Everything is built around efficiency, including what we read in the form of tweets, texts, or captions. So to go back and retrain the ability to calmly and carefully intake information is a good idea. I found myself making a quick initial guess at each of the 10 images in the article, before taking another 20-30 seconds to fully process the scene and make a second guess that each time turned out to be far more accurate to the actual background of the photo.

Best Practices for Overlays

The article on the overuse of overlays by Nielsen Norman Group sheds light on the challenges and considerations in implementing overlays and lightboxes. The writing describes well the delicate balance designers must strike between enhancing user experience and avoiding pitfalls that can lead to frustration and decreased task completion. The great example of Apple's modification to eliminate the overlay in their MobileMe support section underscores the importance of reassessing design choices when overlays hinder user interaction. It serves as a reminder that while overlays can offer elegant solutions, they should not be a default choice and require careful consideration. I think this article serves as a great guide for new designers like myself, urging us to think critically and mindfully about the impact of overlays on user experience.

Form Design Best Practices

As a Graphic Designer, this article on form design resonates with me in terms of the balance between aesthetics and functionality, as it can be a tough mental battle between artistic creativity and a good user experience (UX). For example, the emphasis on minimizing input fields and following the "Less is More" theory aligns with the design principle of simplicity, ensuring a visually clean and efficient form. The article's author, Salim Ansari, cautions against assumptions and highlights the need for thorough user research, a principle applicable to graphic design as well, where understanding the target audience is absolutely crucial. Additionally, I feel that the mention of accessibility considerations serves as a valuable reminder to designers to create designs that cater to users of all abilities.
